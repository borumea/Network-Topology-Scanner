Modern computer networks are becoming increasingly more complex and dynamic, making traditional static methods of topology analysis insufficient for identifying vulnerabilities. Conventional tools rely on predefined rules or manual inspection that may fail to adapt to evolving network conditions or recognize emerging structural risks as netowrks expand. Furthermore, as networks scale and diversify across modern cloud environments or other decentralized architectures, the need for adaptive analysis capable of understanding structural risk patterns becomes significantly more important.

This project explores the application of artificial intelligence in network topology scanning and analysis, focusing on identifying weak points, bottlenecks, and single points of failure using data inferencing rather than static heuristics. By leveraging AI models capable of learning patterns from network structure, performance metrics, and simulated failure scenarios, the proposed approach aims to enhance proactive risk identification and resilience assessment. The central challenge lies in determining whether AI analysis can improve accuracy and predictive capabilities compared to traditional approaches, aiding in creating more robust network architectures.
